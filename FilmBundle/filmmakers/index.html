The Goals

1

To expose your film to a new market and get it seen by as many people as possible.

2

To make you money without compromising your other distribution/marketing channels.

3

To support the film charities and non-profits that have long supported you.

What We Do

Every month, we partner with 5-7 filmmakers and 2 non-profits to bring a limited-time, streamable bundle of amazing independent films to consumers.

The twist? Customers choose how much they want to pay for it. And to take it a step further, they choose how much of their payment goes to the films (including the individual filmmakers if they feel so inclined), to the charities, and even to us. We put all the control in the customers' capable hands.

While this model is new to the film industry, it has been used very successfully in the independent video game space and has raised over $20M for game developers and $10M for charities in the last 2 years.

These numbers are all well and good, but you may be asking yourself one very common question...

What's to stop people from paying $.01 for my work??

Glad you asked! In short, they are free to do so, but if psychology tells us anything, most won't, and besides, we've worked in several hooks to keep prices up. :-)

Pay above average to unlock special content.

We reserve special features and at least one film for customers that pay above the current average price. This encourages higher payments, provides a suggested value, and causes the average to increase as the promotion goes on.

Extreme transparency

We show everybody how many bundles we've sold, the average price, how the money is divided, etc. This empowers our audience, and makes them invested in seeing your film succeed.

Top contributor leaderboard

The top contributors are shown on a leaderboard right on the home page. This encourages competition among cinephiles. Top contributors get access to unique content and opportunities and show their support of indie film. We encourage competition around here. ;)

Two chances to win!

While streaming your film, as the credits roll users have an opportunity to tip the filmmaker if they loved the film. Because customers weren't forced into a set price initially, they are more likely to further support films they loved.

etc...

If doubt still clouds your mind, grasshopper, you can always look to Neil Gaiman, Radiohead, Panera Bread, or THQ. They've all participated in pay what you want promotions.

Questions?

Here are a few common ones, but if yours aren't answered below, shoot me an email.

What about the rights to our films?

We take absolutely no rights to the films and you are free to continue selling them via any avenue you wish. We aim simply to promote your work to a larger audience and help you get the money you deserve. We only require that films not be available online for free during or within 2 months of the promotion.

How are the films distributed?

Customers will be able to stream the films they've purchased by logging in to their secure FilmBundle account. Filmmakers can also choose to make their films available to download if they wish, but that is entirely up to the rights holder.

How do I get paid?

After the promotion ends, we will send you 100% of the payment designated to you minus the 2.8% credit card transaction fee which is taken before we receive payment.

How does promotion work?

We have direct contact with over a hundred bloggers and industry influencers large and small. With each bundle we coordinate a PR push to accompany its release. We promote the bundle by getting buzz around your film and you as a filmmaker.

As experienced internet entrepreneurs we come from a background of SEO and SEM (search engine optimization and marketing), and have the ability to execute tactics that most filmmakers don't.

We built the site from the ground up to encourage sharing of content. We make it extremely easy for customers to tell their friends about it. We know they'll love your work and won't be able to post it to twitter, facebook, pinterest and everywhere else they live online.

Accompanying each launch, are several off-line events (mixer, panels, competitions) to build buzz and anticipation from our soon to be loyal following.

We also rely on you as filmmakers and our non-profit partners to tell your fans about the bundle. That means that hopefully you will have the fan bases of 6 other filmmakers and 2 non-profits looking at your work.

Level with me, how much will I make per sale?

Obviously this will vary from customer to customer, but it is true that while you can make substantial money by being in a bundle, in most cases you won't earn as much per sale as you would selling directly. Due to the flash sale nature of the bundles, we like to think of this as a promotion for the film and you as a filmmaker, rather than a long-term sales channel.

At the end of the day, we know what you really want is to have your work seen and appreciated, so think of it this way - would you rather have your work seen by 100 people at $10 each or 1000 people at $1 each?
