Our Goals

1

To expose amazing independent films that deserve to be seen to a new audience and build the independent film community.

2

To make money for filmmakers so they can keep doing what they do.

3

To give back and support arts driven charities.

What We Do

Every month, we partner with 5-7 filmmakers and 2 non-profits to bring a limited-time, streamable bundle of amazing independent films to consumers.

The twist? Customers choose how much they want to pay for it. And to take it a step further, they choose how much of their payment goes to the films (including the individual filmmakers if they feel so inclined), to the charities, and even to us. We put all the control in the customers' capable hands.

While this model is new to the film industry, it has been used very successfully in the independent video game space and has raised over $20M for game developers and $10M for charities in the last 2 years.

These numbers are all well and good, but you may be asking yourself one very common question...

What's to stop people from paying $.01 for my work??

Glad you asked! In short, they are free to do so, but if psychology tells us anything, most won't, and besides, we've worked in several hooks to keep prices up. :-)

Pay above average to unlock special content.

We reserve special features and at least one film for customers that pay above the current average price. This encourages higher payments, provides a suggested value, and causes the average to increase as the promotion goes on.

Extreme transparency

We show everybody how many bundles we've sold, the average price, how the money is divided, etc. This empowers our audience, and makes them invested in seeing your film succeed.

Top contributor leaderboard

The top contributors are shown on a leaderboard right on the home page. This encourages competition among cinephiles. Top contributors get access to unique content and opportunities and show their support of indie film. We encourage competition around here. ;)

Two chances to win!

While streaming your film, as the credits roll users have an opportunity to tip the filmmaker if they loved the film. Because customers weren't forced into a set price initially, they are more likely to further support films they loved.

etc...
